Project Overview

As part of PiXELL River’s initiative to modernize its digital banking services, this project focused on designing a multi-factor authentication (MFA) system integrated into the institution’s mobile app. The goal was to provide stronger account protection for both individual and business clients—particularly those from Indigenous communities—by introducing secure, flexible, and user-friendly login methods. Our solution was tailored to real user concerns about security, ease of access, and cultural sensitivity.

Design and Development Process

Research and Planning

We began by analyzing PiXELL River’s comprehensive case study, user personas, internal usage reports, and customer feedback. Our key insights included:

  • Individual customers aged 25–45—primarily mobile users—expressed serious concerns about data security and limited digital literacy.
  • Customer D specifically called for two-factor authentication to feel more secure using the mobile app.
  • 15% growth in mobile app usage over the past year suggested a shift toward mobile-first engagement.

We also mapped user journeys and identified the critical friction points—login complexity, lack of flexible options, and the need for security even after logging in. These insights shaped our multi-layered authentication design.

Challenges and Solutions

The challenge was to implement a strong security solution that remained inclusive for users with various devices, internet access levels, and digital skill sets. Here’s how we addressed them:

  • Challenge: Low trust in current security measures.
    Solution: Introduced MFA with multiple entry points—biometric (Face ID/Fingerprint), one-time passcodes via SMS/email/phone call, and 3rd-party authentication apps—to give users control and confidence.
  • Challenge: Sensitive data exposure in public spaces.
    Solution: Implemented post-login visibility toggles for balances and personal information to ensure privacy even during on-the-go banking.
  • Challenge: Device and literacy diversity.
    Solution: Provided options for both advanced and basic devices, paired with onboarding support in the user’s preferred language to simplify setup.

Final Outcome

The prototype presents a practical, secure, and culturally respectful authentication system for PiXELL River’s mobile app. It directly aligns with user needs and modern security standards, all while preserving accessibility. The project demonstrates:

  • Responsive design based on customer feedback
  • Feasibility with current technologies and cost-effective implementation
  • Improved user trust, especially among Indigenous clients in rural and urban areas
  • Enhanced brand credibility through visible commitment to user safety

This project not only solves a technical problem—it builds a bridge between digital innovation and community trust. It’s a testament to how inclusive UX design and secure software development can work hand-in-hand to serve diverse populations.
